# Customer Churn Analysis – Evans PLC

**Telecom Provider – Snapshot Analysis (January 2026)**

---

## Overview

Evans PLC is experiencing an overall churn rate of **~26.9%** (1,796 churned customers out of 6,687 total). The analysis reveals that churn is not random — it is strongly driven by specific, highly actionable customer segments, contract types, and competitive pressures.

---
## Dataset Overview

- **The dataset contains customer-level information including:

Contract type (Monthly, One-Year, Two-Year)

Account length (tenure in months)

Customer service call frequency

Monthly charges

Payment method

Demographic groups

Churn status (Yes/No)

## Most Critical Findings

### 1. Contract Type is the #1 Predictor of Churn
- **Month-to-month contracts** → 46.29% churn rate
- **Yearly contracts (1 or 2 years)** → 6.62% churn rate
- **Key Insight:** Customers on monthly contracts are ~7× more likely to churn

### 2. Senior Citizens are a High-Risk Segment
- Customers aged >60 years show a churn rate of **38.46%** (significantly above average)

### 3. Competitors Are the Leading Stated Reason for Leaving
- **31.59%** of churned customers explicitly cited competitor-related reasons (aggressive promotions, better offers, etc.)

### 4. Surprising Unlimited Data Plan Pattern
- Customers **with** Unlimited Data → 32.11% churn rate
- Customers **without** Unlimited Data → 16.10% churn rate
- **Key Insight:** Heavy/unlimited data users appear to be experiencing dissatisfaction (possible causes: speed/throttling issues, price sensitivity after high usage, or better competitor unlimited offerings)

## Strategic Recommendations

Ordered by estimated potential business impact:

| Priority | Recommendation | Expected Impact | Difficulty | Estimated ROI Timeline |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|------------|------------------------|
| **1** | **Aggressive conversion from monthly → yearly contracts**<br>• Discounts for early conversion<br>• Loyalty bonuses at renewal<br>• Highlight price stability & savings | Very High – could cut churn rate dramatically | Medium | 3–9 months |
| **2** | **Targeted retention program for Senior Citizens (>60)**<br>• Simplified plans/billing<br>• Dedicated senior support line<br>• Easier interface & family assistance features | High – addresses clear demographic vulnerability | Medium | 6–12 months |
| **3** | **Competitive response & win-back campaign**<br>• Identify high-churn states<br>• Match or beat key competitor promotions<br>• Proactive outbound offers to at-risk customers | High – directly addresses #1 churn reason | Medium–High | 3–12 months |
| **4** | **Deep-dive investigation into Unlimited Data customers**<br>• Survey recent churned unlimited users<br>• Analyze usage + complaints + speed/test data<br>• Consider tiered unlimited plans or speed guarantees | Medium–High – explains counter-intuitive finding | Medium | 6–18 months |
| **5** | **Improve customer service experience**<br>• Reduce resolution time<br>• Proactive outreach after 2+ support calls<br>• Better self-service tools | Medium – supports overall satisfaction | Medium | 6–18 months |

## Bottom-Line Business Impact Potential

Implementing strong action on the top 2–3 recommendations (especially **monthly-to-yearly conversion** + **senior retention**) has the realistic potential to:

- Reduce overall churn rate by **8–15 percentage points** within 12–18 months
- Translate to very significant customer retention
- Protect revenue
- Reduce customer acquisition costs
